Ebola - one word, one threat uniting nations.

The Commissioner of Health for the Nigerian state of Lagos, Dr. Olajide Idris, is a physician with experience in both a nation that faced Africa's ebola storm and a nation that was surprised by the fever's arrival.

It is the winning battle against ebola that has been the focus of his nation. And as news reports emphasized, ebola became a stunning challenge to residents and health care professionals in Dallas and the United States.

Dr. Idris, critically involved in the Lagos procedures, is familiar with the ways government and medicine mesh in the United States. After becoming a physician in Nigeria, Dr. Idris earned a Master's Degree in Public Health at Yale University. In the mid-'90s, Dr. Idris continued his medical studies working in emergency medicine and cardiology at stateside schools and hospitals in the United States.

Nigeria has been ebola-free since August 31 after being struck in July when a Liberian-born American came to Lagos after spending time in Liberia. Through swift action, strict quarantine measures and screening of victim contacts, Nigeria managed to stop the virus.

More than 8,200 people have died from ebola and more than 20,700 have fallen ill in the latest outbreak that touched the nations of Sierra Leone, Liberia, Guinea and Nigeria.
